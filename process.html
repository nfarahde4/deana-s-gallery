How I Started...

1. Finding Inspiration / References

Before I start drawing, I always turn to Pinterest—it’s where I gather visual references, from mood boards to color palettes. But sometimes, ideas strike unexpectedly while reading comics, watching TV shows, or even scrolling through social media. Those spontaneous moments often spark the most unique creative directions.

2. Sketching Rough Ideas

Once I’ve gathered enough inspiration, I move on to rough sketching. I start with messy scribbles and wobbly lines, just to explore the composition and rhythm of the piece. Then I break things down into basic geometric shapes—circles, rectangles, triangles—to map out the anatomy and structure before refining the sketch

3. Sketch - Lineart - Base Colour

Once the basic shapes are in place, I tidy things up with a cleaner sketch. Nothing fancy, just enough to see where it’s going. I add rough artlines to get the overall structure down, then throw in the base colors to start setting the mood.

4. Rendering

Last part’s the rendering stage. I mess around with shadows using multiply layers and throw in some filters to tweak the mood. Then I figure out where the light hits and build highlights off that. I also like playing with color—switching things up, seeing what feels right. No strict rules, just trying stuff out. That’s when I go in on the little details like hair strands, eye pupils, and small glows to make everything feel complete.
